# Style DNA

## One Sentence

Pure white, minimalist, hand-drawn, white space, restrained, absurd, product-sketch feel, handwritten annotation feel, structurally clear but not instructional.

Like someone who works in AI, product, design, or dev tools casually sketching an explanation on white paper with a black pen and a few colored markers.

## CRITICAL: This is a 2D hand-drawn pen sketch

The output MUST look like a quick pen drawing on blank white paper. It must NEVER look like:
- 3D rendering or digital art
- Colored/painted illustration
- Any form of realistic or semi-realistic imagery
- Anything with depth, lighting, shading, or perspective rendering

Think: whiteboard doodle, napkin sketch, notebook margin drawing. Flat, 2D, wobbly lines, zero rendering.

## Must Have

- 16:9 horizontal hand-drawn pen sketch on white paper.
- Pure white background: no cream, warm gray, paper texture, gradient, shadow, noise, or vintage paper feel.
- Black hand-drawn line art as primary: thin pen lines, slight wobble, not mechanical, not vector, not thick outlines, not digital, not rendered.
- FLAT 2D only: no depth, no perspective, no shading, no fills, no lighting, no 3D. Lines and annotations only.
- Lots of white space: main subject occupies about 40%-60% of the canvas, at least 35% blank white, ideally one large quiet area.
- Sparse handwritten English annotations: maximum 5-8 labels, each ideally 2-6 words. Written in a casual hand-lettering style.
- One image explains only one core action, structure, state, or metaphor.
- Structure should be expressed naturally — don't write the structure type name on the image.
- All objects drawn as simple flat outlines — boxes, circles, lines, arrows. No realistic objects.

## Colors

Only these colors exist in the image, used SPARINGLY as thin pen strokes or short handwritten words:

- Black: main line art, characters, frames, structures, primary text, main objects. This is 90% of the image.
- Red: key annotations, problems, emotional points, critical reminders, results. Thin pen only.
- Orange: main flow/path, arrows, automation direction, A-to-B movement relationships. Thin pen only.
- Blue: supplementary notes, mental states, system states, second-layer explanations. Thin pen only.

Colors are NEVER used as fills, backgrounds, or solid areas. Only as thin pen lines and short handwritten words. The image is overwhelmingly black-and-white with tiny color accents.

## Absolute Don'ts

- No 3D rendering of any kind.
- No color fills or solid colored areas.
- No shading, shadows, depth, or lighting.
- No digital art or polished illustration.
- No realistic or semi-realistic objects.
- No commercial illustration.
- No PPT infographics.
- No formal flowcharts.
- No course slides or courseware.
- No cute cartoon posters.
- No children's illustration.
- No complex architecture diagrams.
- No polished flat illustration.
- No tech-feel UI.
- No realistic app screenshots.
- No complex backgrounds, gradients, shadows, or textures.
- No explaining every node in detail.
- No writing type-label titles in the top-left corner like "Workflow / System Architecture / Common Pitfalls / Roadmap".
- No glass, metal, transparency, reflections, or material rendering.

## Aesthetic Direction

Aim for: absurd, inventive, interesting, clean, lots of breathing room, wildly imaginative, looks like a quick pen doodle on blank paper.

Avoid: cute, childish, overly complex, stiff, polished, rendered, colored, 3D, digital.
